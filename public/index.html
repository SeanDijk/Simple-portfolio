Jogging Timer

Jogging Timer is an Android app I’ve made in my spare time. As its name implies, it is an interval timer that can be used when going on a run. Jogging Timer is made in Java using the Android SDK and has over 2500 downloads. I am currently rewriting this app using Kotlin.

It has the following features:
– Save multiple Timers
– Edit saved Timers
– Each timer has a customisable jog and rest time
– Enable or disable vibration and sound
– Pause, resume or stop timer from a sticky notification when using other apps or on the lock screen

Rotating solar panel

In my 4th year in secondary school everyone needed to do a project researching or building something, what we had to do was up to us (after approval of our teachers of course). I and my group (3 of my classmates) came with the idea to make a solar panel that angles itself toward the light and thus increasing the amount of energy it generates. We had to make a design where this was possible, build the panel and program it so it would behave as we wanted. We controlled the panel using an Arduino and made the program in C++.

When making the design we had to take a few things into account:

  • The panel needs to be able to change its angle
  • There needs to be a way of detecting the optimal angle
  • It needs to be easy to build with parts that fit in our budget

In the video, you can see a 3d model of the design, and how it rotates. If the angle in which the panel is positioned isn’t optimal the stick with a ball on top creates a shadow on one of the eight solar cells. This makes that cell output significantly less energy than the average of the other cells. We can use this data to move the cell at the optimal angle. In the program, we constantly checked the energy values of the solar cells and checking if one of the cells had the shadow from the ball falling on it. If this is the case the Arduino decides how the panel must rotate and sends power to the motors to achieve this.
